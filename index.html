Work Experience

With a solid academic background in Landscape Ecology and Nature Conservation from Greifswald University, I joined the Forest Research and Training Center (FRTC) as an Assistant Research Officer, initially focusing on the Forest Survey and Carbon Monitoring Section. Here, I played a pivotal role in designing and implementing a comprehensive national forest inventory, and conducted forest data analysis, carbon density assessments and mapping. Additionally, my responsibilities were over time encompassing advanced skills related to calculating emission factors, drafting emission reduction reports, and actively participating in initiatives like the LEAF Program. Currently, as a Research Officer in the Climate Change Management Division at the Ministry of Forest and Environment, I am deeply involved in preparing and submitting national reports on greenhouse gas emissions, as well as mobilizing resources for climate change-related programs.
